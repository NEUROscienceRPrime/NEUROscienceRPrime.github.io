Naloxone vs MOR

How a stiff arm on a nitrogen locks a receptor out of its active conformation — and blocks an overdose.

Key principle: Naloxone and morphine compete for the exact same binding pocket on MOR. Naloxone wins because its Kd (~1.5 nM) is roughly 3× lower than morphine's (~4 nM). Higher affinity = longer average dwell time = dominates at equal concentrations.

Why repeat doses in overdose

Naloxone is short-acting (t½ ~60–90 min). Fentanyl and carfentanil have much longer durations. Once naloxone dissociates, the opioid rebinds and the overdose returns. EMS gives repeat doses not because the first failed — because it wore off.
