What this is

The brain's neurochemistry isn't abstract. If you want presence, peak performance, or to understand why you do the things you do — it breaks down to neuroscience. The perception of other people, the bonding and reward from human connection, the confidence you feel — all of it originates in the brain.

Unless you understand the machinery of your own brain, you won't be able to work with it. Once you can, you will reach a level you once thought was impossible. This isn't about showing how smart you are. It's about self-discovery.

You could have ADHD and take medicine to ease the symptoms — and your own brain will try to counteract the drug, even if it helps you. This is the war being documented here. And it has a larger-than-life meaning.

Real world — Kensington

Go to Kensington, Philadelphia — which I recommend you don't — and you will see stimulant and opioid addicts losing their lives for a drug. Children who felt raised by that substance, not a parent. That's the connection being made here. Why does this happen at a molecular level?

Real world — memory & trauma

Survivors of extreme trauma who couldn't remember the events they faced. They didn't simply forget — the brain was hiding it from them. That's not weakness. That's a survival mechanism running exactly as designed. Your enemies are just pawns the brain uses to checkmate you.
